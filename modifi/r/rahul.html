Grade Point Average (GPA)

GPA is a measure to calculate Individual’s performance in any academic course. Grade is calculated on the basis of relative merit of marks obtained in any particular subject as per the following table.

Criteria Grade Grade Points Grade Definition
Top 10% in Merit O 6 Outstanding
10% - 35% in Merit A 5 Very Good
35% - 65% in Merit B 4 Good
65% - 90% in Merit C 3 Average
25% <= Marks < 33% E 1 Poor
Marks < 25% O 6 Outstanding

Average of all the grades is calculated according to a specified formula (mentioned below). This average of all the Grades is known as Grade Point Average or GPA. GPA / CGPA / SGPA :- Grade Point Average is the average of grades obtained by a student. It is broadly categorized into two types :- 1. SGPA – SEMESTER GPA refers to the average of grades obtained in any particular semester. 2. CGPA – COMMULATIVE GPA refers to average of grades obtained overall from 1st semester to the current semester of the student. Most of the companies consider this during the recruitments.

RULE REGARDING F GRADE IN CALCULATION

Only 5 (O A B C D E) grades are counted while calculating GPA in CCT. If a student obtains F Grade in one or more subjects then the subject is not counted towards the GPA. It is in the favour of students as otherwise an F Grade will drastically harm the GPA for any student.

Formula Used

This SGPA cum CGPA calculator follows the given procedure. All the formulae (written in blue) used are mentioned here for reference.

  1. Grade Points = Grade Obtained * Credits
  2. Total Grade Points are calculated by adding all the grade points.
  3. Total Credit Hours are calculated by adding all the credit hours.
  4. GPA = (Total Grade Points) / (Total Credit Hours)
FOR CALCULATING CGPA 1. Previous CGPA * No. of Semesters passed. (Please note that you have to enter CGPA upto last semester and not the SGPA of last semester) 2. CGPA = {(Previous CGPA * no. of semesters) + Current GPA} / (No. of Semesters + 1)
